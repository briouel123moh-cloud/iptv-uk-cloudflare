## Who actually holds Premier League broadcasting rights in 2026

The current domestic rights cycle runs from 2025/26 through to 2028/29, locked in by a £6.7 billion deal struck in December 2023. Two broadcasters share all live domestic matches between them, roughly 267 to 268 per season. There is no third player in this space, and no streaming service has muscled in during this cycle. For the official list of broadcasters and how the packages are divided, see the Premier League's broadcasters page for full details.

### Sky Sports: the biggest slice of the broadcast calendar

Sky holds rights to a minimum of 215 matches per season across four packages. That covers Saturday evening (17:30), most Sunday fixtures at both 14:00 and 16:30, Monday and Friday evening kickoffs, and two full midweek rounds. When you watch the headline Super Sunday fixture or the Monday night game, you are watching content that only Sky's platform can legally show. With the contract running to 2029, this arrangement is not shifting anytime soon.

### TNT Sports: the Saturday lunchtime specialist

TNT Sports holds Package A, covering roughly 52 matches per season. Their focus is the Saturday 12:30 kickoff slot, which they have owned for years and continued under the new rights cycle. If you still know this channel as BT Sport, that is correct: BT Sport rebranded to TNT Sports in 2023, and since March 2026, TNT Sports moved from the discovery+ platform onto HBO Max. ### What BBC and Amazon Prime actually offer in 2026

BBC provides free-to-air highlights only. There are no live Premier League matches on BBC One, BBC Two, or BBC iPlayer. Full stop. Amazon Prime Video's deal for live Premier League matches ended after the 2024/25 season, and they hold zero live rights in the current cycle. Any article or social media post suggesting Amazon covers live Premier League in 2026 is out of date. Knowing this upfront saves you from signing up for a service expecting matches that simply are not there.

## How can I watch every Premier League match without a Sky subscription?

The short answer is: not through a single traditional streaming service. The rights are contractually split between two separate broadcasters, and that structure is fixed until 2029. If you subscribe to only one, you will miss a meaningful chunk of matches every single week of the season.

### Why the rights split creates a coverage gap

The maths are straightforward. Sky has 215+ matches and TNT has around 52, giving you approximately 267 to 268 live domestic fixtures when you combine both. Subscribe to only NOW TV Sports and you cover Sky's slate, but you miss every TNT match, including all Saturday lunchtime games. Subscribe to only TNT Sports and you cover 52 matches while missing roughly 80 per cent of the season's live fixtures. Neither service alone gets you close to full coverage, and that is a deliberate result of how the rights packages were structured at auction.

### Which service combinations get you closest to full coverage

The realistic traditional route is a NOW TV Sports Month Pass for Sky's matches, combined with TNT Sports access via HBO Max for the remaining fixtures. EE TV's Big Sport bundle packages both into one bill, which is tidier but still reflects the underlying cost of two separate rights holders. Stacking these services is not cheap, and the total monthly figure is something every fan should see clearly before committing. ## Your traditional streaming options and what they actually cost

Concrete numbers matter here. Promotional pricing and introductory offers cloud the real picture, so these are the standard rates as of 2026.

### NOW TV Sports: day pass vs. month pass

The NOW TV Sports Day Pass is £14.99 for 24 hours, which works for the occasional big match but adds up quickly if you use it more than twice a month. The Month Pass sits at £34.99/month and gives you all 12 Sky Sports channels plus Sky Sports+. Full HD (1080p) requires the Boost add-on on top of that. **For Sky's matches alone, you are looking at £34.99/month as a baseline, not including TNT Sports.** The app works on Firestick, Samsung and LG Smart TVs, iOS, and Android, so device compatibility is not a barrier.

### TNT Sports: standalone access options

Since the migration to HBO Max in March 2026, accessing TNT Sports as a standalone subscriber means either a 12-month commitment at £25.99/month or a flexible rolling contract at £33.99/month. Add that to your NOW TV Month Pass and you are spending between £60.98 and £68.98 per month for near-complete Premier League coverage. That is before any Boost add-ons, and it still does not guarantee every single fixture depending on how schedules fall. For most fans, that combined total is the number that sends them looking for alternatives.
