## What live sports is actually costing UK viewers in 2026

Before looking at ways to cut the bill, it helps to understand what the full bill looks like. Most viewers dramatically underestimate how much they're paying across multiple services when they add them up.

### The real monthly cost of Sky Sports and NOW TV

NOW TV offers three main entry points for Sky Sports in 2026: a £14.99 day pass (24 hours, no auto-renew), a £12.99 week pass (seven days), and a rolling monthly at £34.99. There is a 6-month saver deal that brings the equivalent rate down to £9.99 a month, but it auto-renews to £34.99 once the fixed term ends, a detail that catches a significant number of viewers off guard. If you go the full Sky TV route on a 24-month contract, Sky Sports Complete starts at £22 a month during the promotional period before rising to £33 to £36 once that window closes.

### TNT Sports and DAZN: what they add to the total

TNT Sports via Sky costs between £28 and £34 a month depending on whether you're on a contract or rolling basis. DAZN holds boxing and combat sports rights; the Monthly Flex plan sits at £25.99, while the annual commitment drops to £15.99 a month. Stack Sky Sports, TNT, and DAZN together and your monthly bill sits consistently between £70 and £90, before any April price increases land. That context makes every alternative in this guide look considerably more attractive.

## Free-to-air sports: live sports streaming cheap starts here

Free-to-air channels are the most underused tool for cutting a sports bill. Most viewers are already entitled to a substantial amount of live and highlights coverage, it simply requires knowing where to look.

### BBC iPlayer, ITV, and Channel 4: the sports they carry

[BBC iPlayer](https://www.livesoccertv.com/channels/bbc-iplayer/) carries FA Cup coverage up to the semi-final stage, Wimbledon, Test cricket highlights, Six Nations (shared rights), select athletics, and full FIFA World Cup access in 2026. Channel 4 broadcasts F1 highlights for every race weekend, which matters for fans who can follow the season without needing live coverage. ITV covers the Six Nations alongside the BBC, the Tour de France, the Grand National, and selected boxing nights. All three platforms stream on every major device and require nothing beyond a TV licence, this is the non-negotiable starting point for any viewer working to reduce their sports bill.

### How much free-to-air actually covers

Taken together, BBC iPlayer, ITV, and Channel 4 deliver a meaningful portion of the UK's highest-profile annual sporting events at zero extra cost. A viewer who maximises these platforms before paying for anything else is already in a stronger budget position than most. The gaps, live Premier League, live F1, most Champions League, UFC, are where the paid options below come in.

## Short-term passes: the smart move for occasional viewers

For sports fans who follow a handful of key events each year rather than wall-to-wall coverage, short-term passes remove the need for any rolling commitment.

### NOW TV day and week passes: when they make sense

The £14.99 NOW TV day pass covers a full 24 hours across all Sky Sports channels, including live Premier League, F1, and cricket. The £12.99 week pass extends that to seven days. For a viewer who wants to catch one Grand Prix weekend, a cup final, or a single boxing card, a day pass is the most cost-efficient legal option available. If you're watching ten or fewer events a year, day and week passes consistently work out cheaper than committing to a monthly subscription at £34.99. The calculation only flips once you're watching regularly across multiple sports. For details on NOW TV passes and current offers check the official [NOW TV](https://www.nowtv.com/gb) site.

### Free trials and limited-time promos worth watching for

Discovery+ (which carries TNT Sports content) offers a 7-day free trial for new users, check the current Discovery+ terms page, as trial availability and included content can change seasonally. NOW TV regularly runs promotional rates on Entertainment and Cinema passes; new subscriber deals have previously dropped as low as £2.99 for a six-month minimum term, though Sports passes rarely include free trials due to event abuse risk. The strategy of timing a Discovery+ trial to coincide with a Champions League knockout fixture is a legitimate tactic worth keeping in mind. Set a calendar reminder the moment you sign up so you cancel before the auto-renewal charges you at full rate.

## Smart tactics to reduce your sports bill even further

Picking the right primary service does most of the work. These tactics handle the rest, regardless of which route you take.

### Broadband bundles and what they genuinely save

Virgin Media, EE, and Sky all offer sports channels within broadband and TV bundles. Virgin's M350 Sport HD bundle with Netflix runs at £63.99 a month until March 2027, combining broadband, Sky Sports HD, and streaming in one bill. EE's TNT Sports add-on starts at £20 a month on top of a broadband contract, making it a reasonable option if you're primarily a Champions League viewer. The genuine saving in these deals comes from locking into 18 to 24-month contracts, where promotional rates significantly undercut rolling monthly pricing. Always calculate the full 24-month cost rather than just the introductory rate, because most deals revert to a considerably higher price once the initial term ends. Recent reporting also shows providers continue to adjust rates and pass on increases to subscribers, so watch for official headlines such as [Sky to lift prices of Sky Sport and Sky Sport Now](https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/sky-tv-to-lift-prices-of-sky-sport-and-sky-sport-now-by-about-10/CDHAR4JLQNDZPAMPWHL4M5CR44/) when budgeting long-term.

### Seasonal subscriptions, timing, and the split-subscription approach

Subscribe for the season, not the full year. The Premier League runs August to May. F1 runs March to November. If you follow one sport primarily, subscribing only during its active window and cancelling in the off-season cuts your annual spend by two to four months of fees. For households where multiple people watch different sports, one shared IPTV subscription supporting three to five simultaneous streams tends to be significantly cheaper than each viewer holding a separate individual account. This split-subscription sports approach, one account, multiple devices, represents one of the most efficient budget live sports structures available in 2026, particularly when the per-month cost falls below what three NOW TV day passes would total in a single month.
